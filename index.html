⚡ What You Eat Matters!
⚠️
Worries

What challenges might come in the way of making more sustainable food choices? How can we overcome them?

The challenges that may occur are:

  1. Cost — Organic food costs a lot, but is sustainable as it's not factory-made, causing less pollution.
  2. Animals — At least 1 animal is killed every 60 seconds globally, and less than 0.01% of cases are reported.
  3. Workers — Many are overworked with below-median wages. McDonald's makes ~$53B/year while workers earn under $20,000.
  4. Public Health & Food Safety — FSSAI (India) has only $70M vs USDA's $200B, and 500 employees vs 100,000 — yet covers a far larger population.

Stance

What do you think about the current food habits? What steps can you and your family take to be more sustainable?

My family orders organic food, though I recognise this isn't an option for everyone.

Excited

What new ideas or insights did you find interesting? What aspects of food sustainability inspire you?

The most interesting insight was that everything is connected in a cycle. For example, through politics and economics, FSSAI's budget is defined — and with limited funding comes limited resources, which ultimately affects the safety and quality of our food.
